# TimeShift Hivemind — the plain-language vision

This is the north star: what we're building and how it works, with no jargon. When a
decision gets complicated, come back here. If a feature doesn't serve this, question it.

---

**What it is.** Think of all the little instructions that tell an AI assistant how to
behave — your tone, your rules, what it's allowed to say, how it handles your customers.
We're building the *control room* for all of those, across your whole business.

**The problem it fixes.** Right now those instructions get scattered — copied into one
project, tweaked in another, slightly different in a third. Nobody's sure which version is
the real one, and they quietly drift apart until your AI is giving different answers in
different places. It's like having five copies of your company policy in five drawers, all
a bit different.

**How it works — like a good franchise.** There's one master copy of everything, in one
place. Rules flow *downward*: head office to regions to individual stores. The top (you)
sets the non-negotiables, and those can be **locked** so no one underneath can change them.
Each level below customizes only its own patch, within the lane you've given it. And
nothing ever flows back up — a staff member can't change a company rule, a branch can't
change a head-office rule. Authority goes one way: down.

**One rule, one home.** Nothing's allowed to exist in two places. That's the bit that keeps
it honest — the moment you have two copies, you've got two "truths" that drift apart and the
whole thing falls over. So every instruction lives in exactly one spot, and everyone below
simply inherits it.

**Adding something is safe — and we hold your hand the whole way.** When someone wants to
add a new instruction, the system automatically checks it against everything you already
have — does it clash, is it a duplicate, is it secretly different from one you've got? It
flags the issues in plain English and hands the decision to the right person to approve.
Nothing changes until a human ticks the box — and every change is written down: who, what,
when, why.

And we don't leave you to get it right on your own. We guide you the whole way. The screen
only ever asks you for the information that actually belongs to *your* level — so you're
never faced with something above your pay grade or that isn't yours to touch. Between the
way the screens are laid out and the checks running quietly underneath, it's genuinely hard
to make a wrong move at all. The point isn't to catch your mistakes after they happen — it's
to make the right thing the easy thing, and the wrong thing hard to do in the first place.

**You're not locked in.** It doesn't marry you to one AI company. You bring your own AI and
your own keys — you own it, and you can switch providers without anything breaking. The
whole point is that *you* stay in control, not the vendor.

---

**In one line:** it's a single, trustworthy control room for everything your AI is told to
do — where your most important rules can't be quietly overridden, nothing changes without the
right person's say-so, and you can always see why your AI does what it does.

**The spirit of the whole thing:** the easy path and the safe path are the same path. The
screen only shows you what's yours, the checks stop a clash before it lands, and you're only
ever asked for what your level actually needs.
